More About Me

I'm a senior at Virginia Tech graduating in the spring with a Computer Science major. And I'm terrified. I have no clue what I'm doing after I graduate. I still have some time to figure it all out, but less time than I would prefer. I think I want to go into project management, as it blends my computer science knowledge with my interpersonal communication skills."

So, a little bit about me as a person. I'm a twenty year old black woman with blue hair. The hair is something I've wanted to do for years, but was always afraid to because I was terrified of it ruining the shape of my curls (I have type 3c curls). I play Magic: The Gathering, I love dogs (especially greyhounds), I'm a very talkative person and incredibly loud (though I think it's mostly because my voice projects).

I'm a vegetarian (more or less). I won't eat any meats that are farm raised, as the idea of an animal being raised to die bothers me. This came up for me when reasearching puppy mills, as that industry condemns thousands of animals to death annually. I'm a proponent of Adopt Don't Shop, because there are so many animals looking for their forever homes, and the obsession with pure-breeds stigmatize mutts (they're wonderful and have far fewer health issues because of genetic diversity and the lack of inbreeding). I also wish more people spayed and neutered their pets, as that increases the number of pets without a home.

I'm very into politics, and I have very strong opinions. I'm a bleeding-heart liberal and yet anti-feminist (surprising, especially with the blue hair), although it's more of the modern, western, third-wave feminism that I have an issue with. I'm an agnostic atheist, meaning that I don't believe in a god, but I won't make the claim that there is no god. (It's an important to me, although it can confuse people, as agnosticism is often thought of as a religious stance despite it just being a position of knowledge). I love debates(which makes sense with some of my interests), and finding out what people think about things. I try to be respectful, but I know I can come across as a bit harsh, particularly due to my sense of humor.

I have some mental health issues, and have been hospitalized twice because of them. I was diagnosed with bipolar disorder, which my grandmother also had. It's something that I recognize will be a lifelong illness, but I try not to let it affect my life too much.I hope that people with mental health issues know that what they're going through is real, they're not alone, and it does get better.

I love to write. It's something that I've done since I was little, and I see myself continuing to do so throughout my life as a hobby. I think it stems largely from my love of reading when I was a kid. In middle school, I would read a book a day and read Gone with the Wind in three weeks. When I would get in trouble, my parents punished me by taking away my books. As for what I read now, I've taken to some memoirs, my favorite being Furiously Happy by Jenny Lawson. I also love the blog Hyperbole and a Half, as well as the resultant book.

Thanks For Checking This Out!

I hope I didn't waste your time too much. Although if I did, I don't think I really care. Sorry, harsh, I know.
